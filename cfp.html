Call for Contribution:

This workshop aims to bring together researchers, practitioners, and industry experts to discuss the latest advancements and challenges in the field of multi-robot systems, particularly focusing on their applications in logistics and critical infrastructure inspection, especially to investigate advancements in multi-robot systems, including autonomous car fleets, drone swarms for inspection, logistic robot systems and etc. These technologies hold promise for improving automation efficiency and enhancing daily life. However, deploying robotic systems in groups poses challenges such as cost-effective perception implementation, dealing with measurement uncertainties, real-time dynamic planning, and interactions with other agents. Developing such multi-robot perception and navigation systems is often time- and resource-demanding, making this field less explored by the majority of the academic community. This workshop seeks to address this significant domain gap.

Topics of Interest

  • Autonomous car fleets for logistics
  • Drone swarms for infrastructure inspection
  • Logistic robot systems and their applications
  • Cost-effective perception implementation for multi-robot systems
  • Multi-robot navigation under uncertainties
  • Real-time dynamic planning and decision-making in multi-robot systems
  • Sim-to-real transfer in multi-agent systems
  • Coordination and interaction between multiple robotic agents
  • User-friendly and cost-effective simulation environments for multi-robot research
  • Deadlock-free planning strategy for multi-robot systems in logistics and inspection
  • Case studies and real-world applications of multi-robot systems in logistics and inspection

Submission Format

3 to 6 pages, including references. The paper should be in PDF format and use the standard IEEE RAS conference template. Accepted paper will be made available on the website and the authors are invited to make an additionl oral (live) presentations, or a video highlight.
